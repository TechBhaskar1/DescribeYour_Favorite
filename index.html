About

Cricket is an outdoor sport that is liked by most children and they dream of becoming a cricketer at their young age; it is played on a large open field using bat and ball.

It is played between two competing teams consisting of 11 players. It is played in the centre area of a rectangular 22-yard long pitch. The batsman uses it while batting and tries to score runs in the innings.

A member of the opposite team, called a bowler, sends a ball to a member of another team called a batsman. The bowler attempts to hit the ball away from the bat to take a wicket.

A batsman continues his batting until he is evicted by the bowler after making some mistake, whichever team starts batting, they continue batting until ten batsmen are dismissed or one batsman completes a specific six-ball over.

Rules

1. Each team consists of 11 players. These eleven players will have varying roles in the team from batsmen, bowlers, fielders and wicket keepers.

2. The bowler must bowl 6 legal deliveries to constitute an over.

3. Test cricket is played over 5 days where each team has two innings

4. The scores are then cumulative and the team with the most runs after each innings is the winner.

5. International games will have a further two umpires known as the third and fourth umpire. These are in place to review any decisions that the on field umpires are unable to make.
